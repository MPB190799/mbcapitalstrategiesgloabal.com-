Commodity Supercycles Explained: Where Are We Now and What Comes Next?

Commodity supercycles last 15-25 years and are driven by structural under-investment in supply. After a decade of capital starvation in oil, gas, and metals, the current cycle began around 2020 and may have another 5-10 years to run. We map the historical pattern — the 1970s oil shock, the 2000s China boom — against today's conditions. Electrification, re-shoring, and population growth in emerging markets are creating demand that existing supply cannot meet. We identify the commodities with the tightest supply-demand balances and the equities best positioned to compound dividends throughout this cycle.
